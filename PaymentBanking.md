1. Concept & Purpose

Payment Banks are specialized banks introduced to enhance financial inclusion by offering basic banking and payment services.

They focus on low-income households, small businesses, migrant workers, and the unbanked.

Licensed under regulatory bodies (e.g., RBI in India introduced Payment Banks in 2015).

2. Key Features

Deposits: Accept deposits (usually up to a regulatory limit, e.g., â‚¹2 lakh in India per customer).

Payments & Transfers: Enable domestic remittances, mobile payments, UPI, NEFT, IMPS.

No Lending: Payment banks cannot issue loans or credit cards.

Debit Cards / Prepaid Cards: Issued for transactions & withdrawals.

Partnerships: Can distribute insurance, pension, and mutual funds as third-party products.

3. Services Offered

Savings & Current Accounts (digital-first, low KYC barriers).

Mobile Banking & Wallets.

Utility bill payments (electricity, gas, DTH, telecom recharge).

Domestic & cross-border remittances (within regulatory scope).

Government benefit disbursement (DBT, subsidies, pensions).

4. Technology Backbone

Mobile-first platforms (apps, USSD, SMS banking).

API-driven ecosystems (integration with FinTechs & merchant platforms).

Biometric authentication (Aadhaar-based in India).

AI & Analytics for fraud detection and customer insights.

5. Examples

India: Paytm Payments Bank, Airtel Payments Bank, India Post Payments Bank, Fino Payments Bank.

Global Parallels: M-Pesa (Kenya), Alipay (China), PayPal (US, global).

6. Benefits

Drives financial inclusion by reaching rural/unbanked populations.

Provides low-cost, tech-driven banking services.

Reduces dependency on cash and boosts digital economy growth.

7. Challenges

Limited revenue model (since they cannot lend).

High compliance & operational costs.

Competition from traditional banks & FinTechs.
